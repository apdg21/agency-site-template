Phase 01 Precision
Prospecting
1 Look for “trigger events”

Instead of contacting random companies, we focus on businesses that are experiencing changes — those changes usually mean they need help right now.

  • Hiring signals: When a company posts a job for an “Email Marketing Manager” (check LinkedIn or Indeed), it means they have budget and a real need — we can step in before the new hire starts.
  • Tech stack changes: Using tools like BuiltWith, we can see if a company just switched from Mailchimp to Klaviyo or Sendlane. Migrating to a more complex platform is hard — they might need expert help.
  • Funding rounds: Companies that raised a Series A or B (check Crunchbase) just got fresh money and usually have aggressive growth targets — email marketing is a top channel for that.
2 Build a “vulnerability list”

Before reaching out, we do a quick, free audit of their current email setup. We look for simple mistakes that are easy to fix — those become our conversation starters.

  • Missing DMARC/DKIM: These are technical settings that help emails land in the inbox (not spam). If they're missing, their emails probably go to spam — we can point that out.
  • Broken automations: We sign up for their newsletter. If we don't receive a “Welcome email” within 5 minutes, that's a flaw we can mention — it's our foot in the door.
  • Poor segmentation: If they send a “We miss you” discount to someone who just subscribed (that's us), it shows they are not segmenting their audience correctly. We can politely highlight this.
⚡ Why this works: Instead of a generic pitch, we show we've done our homework. These vulnerabilities are proof they need us.
Phase 02 High-Value
Outreach
📨 The “Golden Frame” email structure

Standard cold emails get ignored. We use a format that leads with a useful insight, not a sales pitch. Here is exactly what we send (with placeholders for their company name):

Subject: 15% lift for [Company Name]'s welcome flow?

Hook: “I noticed you're hiring for an Email Lead, but in the meantime, I saw your current abandoned cart flow is missing a SMS hook…”

Free valuable gift: “I recorded a 2‑minute Loom video auditing your checkout emails — here are two quick wins.”

Easy, low‑pressure next step: “Would a 5‑minute chat to see the rest of the audit be useful?”

  • The hook mentions the trigger event (they are hiring) and a specific problem (missing sms in abandoned cart).
  • The value is a micro‑win — we already gave them a free mini audit.
  • The CTA is low friction: just a short chat, not a demo or proposal.
Phase 03 Closing
Proposal
📑 Send a business case, not a service list

When they ask for a proposal, we don't just list what we do. We show them, in simple numbers, how we can increase their revenue. We base it on their actual email list size. Here's an example for a list of 50,000 subscribers:

MetricWhat they get now (approx.)What we aim forExtra monthly revenue
Open rate 22% 35% +$4,500 / month
Click‑through rate (CTR) 1.2% 2.5% +$8,200 / month
Total estimated lift +$12,700 / month

📈 We then show them a simple 90‑day roadmap so they understand exactly what we will do month by month.

  • Month 1 – Foundation: We audit their technical setup, fix deliverability (so emails actually reach inboxes), and build or improve their welcome flow.
  • Month 2 – Optimization: We create advanced audience segments and improve abandoned cart & browse abandonment emails.
  • Month 3 – Scale: We manage weekly campaigns and run A/B tests to continuously improve.
Phase 04 Seamless
Onboarding
🚀 Automated “Success Loop” (no friction)

Losing a client at the last step because of paperwork or delays is painful. We automate everything so the experience is smooth and fast.

1. Digital signature (PandaDoc/DocuSign) 2. Automatic Stripe invoice after signing 3. Intake form via Typeform (asks for brand guidelines, ESP access, goals) 4. Kickoff call scheduled within 48 hours

✅ 2026 client expectation: They want real‑time visibility. We create a dedicated Slack channel or a shared Notion page so they can see progress anytime.
