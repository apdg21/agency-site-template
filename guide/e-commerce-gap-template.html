📦 The "Revenue Gap" outreach

DTC e‑commerce
🎯 Niche: Direct-to-Consumer · Target: Founder, Head of Marketing, E‑commerce Manager
📧 {{Company}}’s abandoned cart flow (and a quick fix)

Hi {{First Name}},

I was just checking out {{Company}} and actually ended up adding the {{Specific Product Name}} to my cart. I didn't finish the checkout because I wanted to see how your "Abandoned Cart" sequence handled the follow-up.

I noticed a couple of things that are likely costing you revenue right now:

The Gap: Your first recovery email arrived 4 hours later. Industry data shows that sending the first touch within 15 minutes usually increases recovery rates by about 20%.

The Miss: The email was text-only and missed a "Dynamic Product Block" to remind me what I almost bought.

I actually put together a 2-minute video audit showing exactly how I’d restructure this flow (and two others) to help {{Company}} capture the ~70% of shoppers who leave without buying.

Worth a 5-minute chat to see the audit?

Best,
{{Your Name}}
{{Agency Name}}

✅ Why this works (The 2026 Strategy)

  • The "In-Basket" Proof: You actually interacted with their store — proves you're not a bot.
  • The Micro-Audit: Pointing out a 4‑hour delay establishes instant authority.
  • The Loom Strategy: Mentioning a "video audit" creates curiosity — it's a gift, not a pitch.
  • The Low-Bar CTA: You ask to show an audit, not a "discovery call."
🎥 Pro‑Tip: The "Loom" Follow‑Up

If they reply "Yes," don't just send a link. Record a quick screen-share (Loom or similar) where you show their website, show a successful competitor's flow, and mock up what their email could look like if redesigned.
