Find the revenue leaks before you pitch

Ghost shopping + technical health + the helpful‑peer approach
🔎 To find a "hole" in a potential client's email strategy, think like a customer first and a forensic auditor second. You are looking for Revenue Leaks—places where money falls out because of poor automation or technical neglect.

1 The "Ghost Shopping" Audit (Front-End)

The easiest way to find immediate gaps without backend access.

📭 The Opt-In Test

Does a pop-up appear? Is it delayed (good) or immediate (annoying)? Does it offer a lead magnet?

🚫 No pop-up = 60% less list growth

👋 The Welcome Flow

Sign up. Do you get an email within 2 minutes? If it takes an hour, they lose the "moment of highest intent."

⏳ Delayed welcome = cold leads

🛒 Cart Abandonment

Add item, enter email, close tab. Do they email within 1 hour?

⚠️ Missing #1 revenue automation

👀 Browse Abandonment

Look at a product for 30 seconds, then leave. Do they email about that specific product?

📉 No behavioral triggers = missed sales

2 Technical "Health Check" (Back-End)

Use free tools like MxToolbox, DNSChecker, or Mail-Tester.com to check deliverability from the outside.

🔐 Authentication (SPF/DKIM/DMARC)

Google & Yahoo strictly block senders without these records.

If DMARC isn't "p=quarantine" or "p=reject", emails hit Promo/Spam.

📧 The "Promo Tab" Scan

Are their emails 90% images and 10% text?

Image-heavy triggers spam filters, slow to load.

3 The "Right Approach" to Handling the Lead

Never say "your email is bad." Use the Lost Opportunity approach.

📨 The "Helpful Peer" Method

"Hi {{Name}}, I was checking out your store and noticed something interesting. I signed up for the list but didn't receive a welcome code for nearly two hours.

Usually, for brands at your scale, that delay results in about a 12-15% drop in first-purchase conversions because the 'buying itch' fades. I’ve seen this happen with other brands in {{Niche}}, and it’s usually just a simple API delay or a trigger setting.

I’d be happy to show you how to tighten that up so you aren't leaving that revenue on the table. No strings attached—just thought you'd want to know."
